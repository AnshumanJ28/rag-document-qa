Acme Robotics — Q3 2026 Internal Summary

Financial Performance
Acme Robotics reported revenue of 82 million dollars in Q3 2026, an increase
of 14 percent compared to the same quarter last year. Operating costs were
reduced by 6 percent following the consolidation of two regional warehouses.
Gross margin improved to 41 percent, up from 37 percent in Q2 2026.

Product Updates
The company shipped its third-generation warehouse picking robot, the
Acme P3, in August 2026. The P3 line uses a new lidar-based navigation
system that reduced average pick time from 9.2 seconds to 6.8 seconds per
item. Customer pilots are currently running in four distribution centers
across Texas and Ohio.

Hiring and Operations
Headcount grew from 410 to 462 employees during the quarter, with most new
hires joining the firmware and field-service teams. The company opened a
new field-service hub in Columbus, Ohio to support the growing number of
P3 deployments in the Midwest.

Risks and Outlook
Management flagged ongoing supply constraints on lidar sensors as the
primary risk to Q4 shipment targets. The company expects Q4 revenue to grow
between 8 and 12 percent year over year, assuming sensor supply stabilizes
by November 2026.
