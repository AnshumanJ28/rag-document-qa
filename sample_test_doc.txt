Project Atlas — Internal R&D Report

Overview
Project Atlas is an internal research initiative exploring low-power
sensor networks for precision agriculture. The project began in
February 2025 and is led by Dr. Elena Vasquez, with a core team of
seven engineers split between the Austin and Lisbon offices. The
total approved budget for the project is 1.8 million dollars over
eighteen months.

Methodology
The team deployed 240 prototype soil sensors across three test farms
in central Texas. Each sensor measures soil moisture, temperature, and
nitrogen levels every fifteen minutes and transmits readings over a
LoRaWAN network with an average range of 6 kilometers. Battery life on
the third-generation sensor board reached 14 months under field
conditions, up from 5 months on the first prototype.

Results
Early results show a 22 percent reduction in water usage on fields
using Atlas-guided irrigation schedules compared to fields using
traditional fixed-schedule irrigation. Crop yield on the test plots
increased by 9 percent during the Spring 2026 growing season. False
positive readings, where a sensor reported abnormal nitrogen levels
that did not reflect true soil conditions, dropped from 12 percent in
the first sensor generation to under 3 percent in the third
generation after a firmware update in March 2026.

Team and Operations
The Austin team focuses on hardware design and field testing, while
the Lisbon team handles the backend data pipeline and the farmer-facing
mobile app. The mobile app currently has 340 active farmer accounts
across four states. Customer support response time averages 6 hours
during planting season and 18 hours otherwise.

Risks and Next Steps
The primary technical risk is sensor corrosion in high-humidity
conditions, which caused an 8 percent failure rate in the Gulf Coast
test plots during the first year. A redesigned enclosure is currently
in testing and is expected to reduce corrosion-related failures to
under 2 percent. The team plans to expand to ten additional farms by
the end of 2026 and is evaluating a partnership with two regional
agricultural cooperatives to fund that expansion.
